# Business Problem

## Scenario
A retail company saw revenue drop by 12% over two quarters after introducing a price increase, reducing promotional intensity, and shifting customer acquisition traffic away from high-converting channels.

## Pain Points
- Revenue declined in both the online and retail store channels.
- Repeat purchase frequency dropped among existing customers.
- Customer support tickets increased around failed deliveries and delayed refunds.
- Leadership wanted a data-backed recovery plan rather than broad discounting.

## Key Questions
- Which customer segments showed the sharpest decline in spend or retention?
- What product categories and channels should be prioritized for recovery?
- Which intervention strategy offers the best balance of revenue recovery and margin protection?
- How should success be measured after the plan is launched?

## Success Criteria
- Improve 90-day repeat purchase rate by at least 8%
- Recover 5% of lost revenue within the next quarter
- Reduce churn in the highest-risk segment by 10%
- Provide a dashboard that executives can review weekly
