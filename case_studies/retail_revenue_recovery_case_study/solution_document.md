# Solution Document

## Analytical Approach
The solution combines business problem framing, SQL-based analysis, and stakeholder-friendly reporting.

### 1. Data Preparation
- Standardize transaction dates and customer IDs
- Classify purchases by channel, product category, and customer segment
- Build a customer-level view that supports churn and repeat-purchase analysis

### 2. Core Analysis
- Compare revenue trends before and after the policy change
- Measure repeat-purchase behavior and churn risk by segment
- Identify products and channels with the greatest revenue sensitivity
- Estimate the likely impact of targeted retention offers

### 3. Recommended Actions
- Prioritize high-value customers with declining spend patterns
- Use targeted offers for customers who are close to churn but have strong historical value
- Increase promotional support only in the top-performing channels
- Improve support experience for customers impacted by delivery and refund issues

### 4. Executive Summary Template
- Revenue is down primarily in low-frequency and price-sensitive segments.
- High-value customers are more resilient, but their repeat rate has weakened.
- Recovery should focus on targeted retention rather than blanket discounting.
- The most effective interventions are channel-specific and segment-specific.

### 5. KPI Framework
- Monthly revenue trend
- Repeat purchase rate
- Churn rate by segment
- Average order value
- Recovery campaign uplift
- Customer lifetime value by segment
